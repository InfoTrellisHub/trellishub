5 Warning Signs of a Hidden Plumbing Leak

📅 3 March 2025 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ ProFix Plumbing Team

A hidden plumbing leak can cause thousands of rands in structural damage — rotting joinery, mould in walls, weakened foundations — long before a single drop of water appears on a visible surface. By the time the damage is obvious, the repair bill is already significant.

The good news is that leaks almost always leave clues. Here are the five signs that a licensed plumber should come and inspect your property:

1

Your water meter moves when nothing is running

Turn off every tap, appliance, and irrigation line in your home. Then go to your water meter and check if the dial is still moving. If it is, water is flowing somewhere in the system — and since everything is off, it's flowing out of a leak. This is the single most reliable DIY check for a hidden leak, and it takes less than five minutes.

2

Unexplained increases in your water bill

If your household's usage habits haven't changed but your municipal water bill has crept up over the past few months, a slow hidden leak is the most likely culprit. Even a pinhole leak in a pressurised pipe can waste hundreds of litres per day. Compare your current bills to the same period last year — a consistent upward trend without explanation warrants investigation.

3

Damp patches, staining, or bubbling on walls or ceilings

Yellowish-brown staining on a ceiling, soft or bubbling paint on a wall, or a patch that feels cool and damp to the touch are all signs of water accumulating behind the surface. These marks typically appear directly below or adjacent to a leaking pipe or fitting. Even if the patch dries out and seems to go away, the underlying leak is still there — and so is the accumulated moisture, which will soon become mould.

4

A persistent musty or damp smell in a room

Mould and mildew have a distinctive earthy, musty odour that's very hard to mask with ventilation or air fresheners. If a particular room — especially a bathroom, kitchen, or room with an external wall — smells persistently damp even when dry, there's a strong chance moisture is trapped behind a wall cavity or under flooring. Left untreated, mould becomes a health hazard and the remediation bill grows substantially.

5

Soft, warped, or discoloured flooring

Wooden floors that have begun to warp, buckle, or show dark discolouration may be absorbing water from a leaking pipe below. Vinyl or laminate flooring that has lifted at the edges or seams, or tiles that have cracked without obvious impact, can also indicate moisture movement beneath the surface. A leak under a slab can go undetected for months because the water spreads laterally before surfacing.

The meter test: Turn off all water in the home, note your meter reading, wait 30 minutes without using any water, and check again. Any movement confirms a leak somewhere in the system.

Why hidden leaks are more expensive than visible ones

A dripping tap is annoying and wastes water, but it's immediately visible and simple to fix. A pipe leaking inside a wall cavity or below a concrete slab is a very different problem. By the time the leak becomes externally visible, water has typically been infiltrating structural materials — timber, insulation, drywall — for weeks or months. Replacing water-damaged structural elements costs far more than the plumbing repair itself.

In Gauteng's older housing stock — particularly homes built in the 1970s and 80s — galvanised steel pipes are common. These corrode from the inside out over decades, developing pinhole leaks that are impossible to detect visually. A plumber can use pressure testing to identify these failures before they become emergencies.

What a plumber will do

A licensed plumber investigating a suspected hidden leak will typically:

  • Perform a pressure drop test on the entire system to confirm a leak exists
  • Isolate sections of pipework to locate which zone the leak is in
  • Use acoustic listening equipment or thermal imaging to pinpoint the source without unnecessary cutting
  • Provide a written quote for the repair before opening any surfaces

Don't delay if you suspect a slab leak. Water infiltrating a concrete foundation can undermine the structural integrity of the slab over time. A plumber should assess this as a priority, not a "wait and see" situation.
